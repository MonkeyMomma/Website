Living With Invisible Illness

Perhaps the hardest part of living with an invisible illness is the simple fact that it is invisible. What that means is, there are no clear outward signs that we are disabled. Paralyzed people have wheelchairs, which make it clear to the world they are disabled. People with Down's Syndrome have the facial features that are common with the condition to indicate their condition to the world. Not that I am complaining that those of us with invisible illnesses aren't in a wheelchair or in some other way clearly disabled. But, since we "look" healthy, or healthy-ish, people often treat us poorly when we are limited by our conditions.

I have had strangers be rude to me when I am moving slow. I've had strangers say nasty things when I have to sit and rest. Things like if I "just dieted" I wouldn't be "so fat" and have to sit down. No one I don't like most people. I eat healthier than most people I know. Because of a medical condition, I have had a constant struggle with weight. But, my weight was actually never an issue that held me back from anything in life. It wasn't until I developed the fibromyalgia that I started having to take rest breaks and not being able to move at a normal speed. So, basically, I just wish people would be nicer to others, in general.

Fibro steals your life

The symptoms of fibromyalgia can literally steal your life. I have lost my career, my favorite sport, my ability to fully enjoy my favorite place in the world... I miss out on family events because I'm too tired. I can't always get housework done in a timely manner, because I am usually in too much pain, or I am too exhausted to finish. I can't finish projects in a timely manner. Things with deadlines, I have to start way ahead. There is so much that it has stolen from me, it is overwhelming. And, when others don't recognize that and treat me rudely or get irritated with me for it, it makes me feel even worse. That's why I think it's so important for people to know about the symptoms of conditions people they care about. And, it's especially important not to be a dick to strangers, because you don't know what they may be going through.
